## Invention Entry #001 — Privacy-First Encounter System

**Date:** July 4, 2026  
**Topic:** PocketPass  
**Status:** Research Phase  

### Problem

People lose valuable real-world connections after events because exchanging contact information is awkward, forgotten, or never happens.

### Proposed Solution

A privacy-first encounter system using nearby device detection and user-controlled identity sharing.

The system should allow users to discover that they crossed paths without automatically exposing private information.

### Why It May Be Novel

Potential novelty may come from the exact combination of:

- Temporary rotating identifiers
- User-controlled public profile cards
- Event-specific profile modes
- Encounter history
- Mutual unlock consent
- Meaningful encounter scoring

### Prior Art to Investigate

- Nintendo StreetPass
- Bluetooth-based social networking patents
- Dating apps using proximity or crossed-path logic
- Apple AirDrop and NameDrop
- Android Nearby Share / Quick Share
- Event networking apps

### Next Steps

- Research existing patents.
- Define technical architecture.
- Identify what is genuinely different.
- Discuss with a patent attorney if the implementation becomes specific and novel.
